# Orlando Main Streets – Image Index

This file lists all district program maps in this repository, with a description of each image.

---

## 1. Audubon Park Garden District
**File:** `audubon-park-garden-district-map.png`  
**Date:** August 2016  
**Dimensions:** 1000 × 1396 px

A program map for the Audubon Park Garden District Main Street, showing the target area along **Corrine Drive** — a tree-lined, walkable commercial corridor in the heart of the Audubon Park neighborhood in northeast Orlando. The highlighted boundary covers several residential blocks surrounding the commercial strip. Nearby landmarks include Audubon Park, Lake Sue, Lake Rowena, Lake Cay Dee, and Lake Druid Park. The district is known for its independent boutiques, restaurants, and community-focused identity as "The Garden District."

## 2. City District
**File:** `city-district-map.png`  
**Date:** August 2025  
**Dimensions:** 1289 × 962 px

A map of the **City District** in downtown Orlando, showing a compact target area in the central business district. The boundary runs roughly from Robinson Street in the north to Anderson Street in the south, and from I-4 in the west to Orange Avenue in the east. Lake Eola is visible to the northeast. The City District encompasses the dense urban core of downtown, including government buildings, offices, and entertainment venues.

---

## 3. The Drive – College Park Main Street
**File:** `the-drive-college-park-main-street-map.png`  
**Date:** February 2026  
**Dimensions:** 1700 × 2200 px

A program map for **The Drive**, College Park's Main Street corridor along **Edgewater Drive** in the College Park neighborhood northwest of downtown Orlando. The target corridor runs in a north–south orientation, flanked by Lake Sarah and Lake Daniel to the northwest and Lake Ivanhoe to the southeast. Other nearby lakes include Lake Silver and Lake Adair. The area also includes Dubsdread Golf Course and Dartmouth Park. College Park is known for its bungalow homes and locally owned shops and restaurants along Edgewater Drive.

---

## 4. Gateway Orlando Market Street
**File:** `gateway-orlando-market-street-map.png`  
**Date:** March 2021  
**Dimensions:** 2183 × 3190 px

A program map for the **Gateway Orlando Market Street** district, displayed in two panels: the section from Brosche Road to Pershing Avenue, and the section from Pershing Avenue to SR-528. The target area follows a long commercial corridor along **Orange Blossom Trail (US-441)** in east Orlando, running south from near Lake Barton through communities with access to Lake Prentice, Airport Lakes Park, Barber Park, and Lake George. The Gateway district serves a diverse, densely populated corridor with a mix of retail, food, and service businesses.

---

## 5. Ivanhoe Village Main Street
**File:** `ivanhoe-village-main-street-map.png`  
**Date:** October 2020  
**Dimensions:** 2198 × 3099 px

A program map for the **Ivanhoe Village Main Street** district just north of downtown Orlando. The large target area centers on the **Orlando Avenue / Edgewater Drive corridor** and surrounds a chain of lakes: Lake Winyah, Lake Estelle, Lake Formosa, and Lake Ivanhoe. Key anchors include the Orlando Cultural Park (home to the Orlando Museum of Art and Orlando Science Center) and Orlando Loch Haven Park & Neighborhood Center. Mathews Park and Lake Rowena are at the northern edge, and Lake Concord and Lake Ivanhoe Park anchor the south. Ivanhoe Village is celebrated for its antique shops, galleries, dining, and arts venues.

---

## 6. The Milk District Main Street
**File:** `milk-district-main-street-map.png`  
**Date:** October 2020  
**Dimensions:** 2190 × 3250 px

A program map for **The Milk District Main Street**, a creative neighborhood east of downtown along **East Colonial Drive (SR-50)**. The target area runs from roughly Primrose Avenue in the west to Woodward Street in the east, bounded by Lake Druid Park to the north and Orlando Main Park / Festival Park to the east. The district takes its name from T.G. Lee dairy (a local dairy still operating nearby) and is home to an eclectic mix of bars, music venues, vintage shops, and independent restaurants. The Colonialtown neighborhood overlaps with the northern portions of the map.

---

## 7. Mills 50 Main Street
**File:** `mills-50-main-street-map.png`  
**Date:** October 2020  
**Dimensions:** 2279 × 3165 px

A program map for the **Mills 50 Main Street** district, named for its location at the intersection of **Mills Avenue and Colonial Drive (SR-50)**. The district covers a large and diverse area stretching from Lake Ivanhoe and Lake Highland in the north to Lake Eola Park in the south, and incorporating Colonialtown, the Colonialtown Neighborhood Center, Orlando Cultural Park, Lake Formosa Park, Big Tree Park, and Park Lake Park. Mills 50 is widely recognized as one of Orlando's most vibrant and multicultural districts, with a thriving collection of Asian restaurants, independent businesses, and creative establishments.

---

## 8. Parramore Main Street
**File:** `parramore-main-street-map.jpg`  
**Date:** January 2022  
**Dimensions:** 2550 × 3300 px

A program map for the **Parramore Main Street** district, a historically significant African-American neighborhood immediately west of downtown Orlando. The map shows two distinct zones: **Zone 1** (green) covers the southern portion of the neighborhood, and **Zone 2** (orange) covers the northern portion near Lake Dot Park. I-4 forms the eastern boundary. The district includes the Dr. J.B. Callahan Neighborhood Community Center, the Orlando Downtown Performing Arts Campus, and is anchored by key streets such as Parramore Avenue. The area is undergoing revitalization while preserving its cultural heritage.

---

## 9. SoDo District Main Street
**File:** `sodo-district-main-street-map.png`  
**Date:** January 2017  
**Dimensions:** 2254 × 3165 px

A program map for the **SoDo (South of Downtown Orlando) District Main Street**, a large residential and commercial area directly south of downtown. The target area encompasses a broad swath of south Orlando bordered by multiple lakes: Lake Davis, Lake Lancaster, Lake Copeland, Lake Of The Woods, Lake Lurna, Crystal Lake, Crystal Lake Neighborhood Park, and Lake Pineloch to the south. The Wadeview Park and Community Center sits in the interior of the district. SoDo features a mix of mid-century neighborhoods, retail corridors, and the prominent SoDo shopping center along Orange Avenue.

---

## 10. Thornton Park Main Street
**File:** `thornton-park-main-street-map.png`  
**Date:** October 2020  
**Dimensions:** 2191 × 3172 px

A program map for the **Thornton Park District (TPD) Main Street**, an upscale historic neighborhood immediately east of downtown Orlando. The target area wraps around **Lake Eola** and Lake Eola Park, Orlando's iconic urban park and amphitheater. The district extends from Livingston Street in the north to South Street in the south, and from Magnolia Avenue in the west to Summerlin Avenue in the east, including Constitution Green and the surrounding residential streets. Thornton Park is known for its brick-lined streets, craftsman bungalows, outdoor dining, and the Sunday Farmers Market at Lake Eola.

---

## 11. West Lakes Market Street
**File:** `west-lakes-market-street-map.jpg`  
**Date:** October 2021  
**Dimensions:** 1700 × 2200 px

A program map for the **West Lakes Properties Market Street** district, a large community in west Orlando. The target area covers a broad residential and commercial zone stretching from W. Colonial Drive in the north to Grand Street in the south, and from near John Young Parkway in the east to Kirkman Road in the west. The district is defined by its many lakes, including Dick Lake, Lake Lorna Doone, Lake Sunset, and Lake Conway, along with the Lorna Doone Park and the John N. Jackson Neighborhood Garden and Pool. The Westgate/Bel Air neighborhood borders the eastern edge. West Lakes serves a diverse population and focuses on economic development along its commercial corridors.
